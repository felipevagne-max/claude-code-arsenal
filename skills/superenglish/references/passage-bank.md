# Passage Bank — English Reading Skill

Use these passages as templates and inspiration for exercises. Adapt, extend, or create similar ones based on the user's level and progress.

## Level 1 — Foundations (A1-A2)

### Passage 1.1 — The Dark Room
> The man walked into the dark room. He could see nothing. Then he found a small light on the table. He picked it up and looked around. The room was empty, but there was a door on the other side.

**Key vocabulary**: walked, dark, nothing, found, light, picked up, looked around, empty, door, other side
**Grammar focus**: Past simple, basic prepositions

**Questions**:
1. What did the man find on the table? (literal)
2. Was the room full of things? (literal)
3. What do you think the man will do next? (inferential)

### Passage 1.2 — The Old Sword
> The boy looked at the old sword on the wall. It was very big and very heavy. His father said, "One day, this sword will be yours." The boy smiled. He wanted to be strong like his father.

**Key vocabulary**: sword, wall, heavy, father, one day, yours, smiled, strong
**Grammar focus**: Past simple, possessive pronouns, adjectives

### Passage 1.3 — The Market
> Every morning, the woman went to the market. She bought bread, fruit, and sometimes fish. The market was always busy and loud. She liked the colors and the smells. It was her favorite part of the day.

**Key vocabulary**: every morning, market, bought, bread, fruit, sometimes, busy, loud, favorite
**Grammar focus**: Past simple regular/irregular, frequency adverbs

## Level 2 — Building Blocks (B1)

### Passage 2.1 — The Soldier's Journey
> The soldier had been walking for three days when he finally saw the walls of the city. His armor was heavy, and his legs ached with every step. But the sight of those tall stone walls gave him the strength to continue. He knew that inside, people were waiting for him — people who depended on the news he carried.

**Key vocabulary**: soldier, armor, ached, sight, strength, continue, depended, carried
**Grammar focus**: Past perfect continuous, relative clauses, cause/effect

**Questions**:
1. How long had the soldier been walking? (literal)
2. Why did seeing the walls give him strength? (inferential)
3. What kind of news do you think he carries? (evaluative)

### Passage 2.2 — The Apprentice
> She had never held a weapon before today. The training master placed the wooden sword in her hands and stepped back. "Attack me," he said calmly. She swung the sword with all her strength. He moved to the side without effort, and she fell forward, hitting the ground hard. "Again," he said.

**Key vocabulary**: apprentice, weapon, training master, wooden, calmly, swung, effort, fell forward
**Grammar focus**: Past perfect, direct speech, adverbs of manner

### Passage 2.3 — The Storm
> The storm arrived faster than anyone expected. Within minutes, the sky turned from blue to black, and the wind began to howl through the narrow streets. People ran for shelter, pulling children behind them. Only the guards remained outside, their cloaks whipping in the wind as they struggled to close the massive iron gates.

**Key vocabulary**: arrived, expected, within minutes, howl, narrow, shelter, pulling, remained, whipping, struggled, massive
**Grammar focus**: Past simple vs. past continuous, participial phrases

## Level 3 — Intermediate Reader (B2)

### Passage 3.1 — Mistborn Style: The Mists
> The mists came every night, rolling through the streets like a living creature hunting for prey. Most people feared them — tales of those who ventured out after dark and never returned were common enough to keep the wise indoors. Yet Vin had learned long ago that the mists were not her enemy. They were her cloak, her shield, her freedom. When the mists rose, she became invisible, and invisibility was the greatest weapon a thief could possess.

**Key vocabulary**: mists, rolling, creature, hunting, prey, feared, ventured, common, wise, cloak, shield, invisible, weapon, thief, possess
**Grammar focus**: Participial phrases, metaphor, parallel structure

**Questions**:
1. Why do most people stay indoors at night? (literal)
2. "They were her cloak, her shield, her freedom" — what does this mean? (literary)
3. What does this passage tell us about Vin's character? (inferential)
4. How does the author create a sense of danger and then subvert it? (evaluative)

### Passage 3.2 — Political Intrigue
> The council had been arguing for hours, and Lord Renoux could see that no resolution would come today. Each house defended its own interests with practiced eloquence, weaving half-truths into arguments that sounded noble but served only greed. He had seen this performance a hundred times before. The real decisions, he knew, were never made in this chamber. They were made in whispered conversations in dark hallways, sealed with promises that would be broken before the ink was dry.

**Key vocabulary**: council, arguing, resolution, defended, interests, eloquence, weaving, half-truths, noble, greed, performance, chamber, whispered, sealed, promises, broken, ink
**Grammar focus**: Past perfect, passive voice, complex sentences, idiomatic expressions

### Passage 3.3 — Magic System
> Allomancy worked through metals. A Mistborn could ingest small amounts of specific metals and then "burn" them internally, drawing upon their power. Each metal granted a different ability: steel let you Push against nearby metals, iron let you Pull them toward you. Tin enhanced the senses, making every sound sharper and every light brighter. Pewter strengthened the body, granting superhuman endurance and speed.

**Key vocabulary**: ingest, specific, internally, drawing upon, granted, ability, enhanced, senses, strengthened, endurance
**Grammar focus**: Passive voice, gerunds, technical description, colon usage

## Level 4 — Advanced Reader (C1)

### Passage 4.1 — Exposition and World-Building
> Allomancy, it appeared, was an art accessible only to the nobility — a fact that the Lord Ruler's propaganda machine reinforced with tireless efficiency. The skaa, ground beneath centuries of oppression, had neither the knowledge nor the audacity to question why this should be so. It was simply the way of things, as immutable as the ashfalls that choked the land or the red sun that bled its light across a dying sky. And yet, in the quiet corners where the Lord Ruler's gaze did not reach, there were those who whispered of change.

**Key vocabulary**: accessible, nobility, propaganda, reinforced, tireless, efficiency, oppression, audacity, immutable, ashfalls, choked, bled, whispered
**Grammar focus**: Appositive phrases, multi-clause sentences, subjunctive mood, literary metaphor

### Passage 4.2 — Internal Monologue
> Kaladin stared at the chasm floor far below, and for a moment — just a brief, treacherous moment — he wondered what it would feel like to simply let go. The thought disgusted him even as it tempted him. He had sworn to protect, to fight, to endure. But oaths made in the light of day sometimes felt meaningless in the crushing darkness of a highstorm night, when the wind screamed and the stones wept and every breath felt like a negotiation with despair.

**Key vocabulary**: chasm, treacherous, disgusted, tempted, sworn, protect, endure, oaths, meaningless, crushing, screamed, wept, negotiation, despair
**Grammar focus**: Conditional, em-dash usage, personification, internal conflict expression

## Level 5 — Near-Fluent (C2)

### Passage 5.1 — Philosophical/Theological
> "The question is not whether a god can die," Hoid said, settling back against the stone wall with the casual ease of someone discussing the weather. "The question is what happens to the divine power when the vessel that held it shatters. Power, you see, does not simply vanish. It transforms. It seeks. And those who understand this — truly understand it, not merely comprehend it intellectually but feel it in their very Investiture — they are the ones who will shape what comes next. The rest of us? We are merely witnesses to the cosmere's endless reinvention of itself."

**Key vocabulary**: vessel, shatters, transforms, seeks, merely, comprehend, intellectually, Investiture, witnesses, cosmere, reinvention
**Grammar focus**: Nested clauses, rhetorical structure, abstract reasoning, em-dash parentheticals

## Vocabulary Exercise Templates

### Template: Context Clues
Present a sentence with one underlined word. Ask the user to guess the meaning from context before revealing the translation.

> "The soldiers retreated behind the fortress walls, their faces **gaunt** from weeks of siege."
> What do you think "gaunt" means? Think about what weeks of siege would do to someone.
> Answer: magro, abatido — thin and exhausted from hardship.

### Template: Word Families
Show how one root generates multiple words:
- **strength** (noun) → **strong** (adj) → **strengthen** (verb) → **strongly** (adv)
- **destroy** (verb) → **destruction** (noun) → **destructive** (adj) → **indestructible** (adj)

### Template: False Cognates Alert
Words that look like Portuguese but mean something different:
- **actually** ≠ atualmente (means "na verdade")
- **pretend** ≠ pretender (means "fingir")
- **push** ≠ puxar (means "empurrar" — the opposite!)
- **fabric** ≠ fábrica (means "tecido")
- **attend** ≠ atender (means "comparecer/participar")

### Template: Sanderson-Specific Terms
Words that have special meaning in Sanderson's universe:
- **Investiture**: the magical energy system of the cosmere
- **Shard**: a piece of divine power held by a god-like being
- **Cognitive Realm**: the world of thought and perception
- **Connection**: a metaphysical bond between people, places, or things
